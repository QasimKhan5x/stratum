# Stratum — Demo Premise (Locked)

This is the finalized premise the SeedAgent, the four specialists, the baseline
comparison, and the recorded demo will all run against. Locked after an
explicit legibility/fit review — see rationale below the premise itself.

---

## Title

**Tideglass Reach**

## One-paragraph premise (goes into the demo's premise field verbatim)

> Tideglass Reach: a coastal city, drowned for two generations, has just
> resurfaced after an unexplained "long ebb." The Tideglass Guild and the Hush
> Choir are racing to claim it before the other does — and nobody agrees on
> whether the bell tone divers keep hearing near the Cathedral Spire is a
> hazard, a hoax, or a warning.

## Scarce resource

**Tideglass** — a fused, glass-like mineral formed when the old city's kilns
and glassworks were flash-submerged. Valuable for lenses, wards, and currency.
Extractable only from specific ruin-hexes during narrow low-tide windows —
this gives the Architect a citable, specific reason to reject a proposal
("that hex is submerged at the proposed access time"), not just an empty-cell
lookup.

## Named factions (trimmed to two, deliberately)

- **The Tideglass Guild** — salvage-industrialists. Wants the Bell debunked
  as a natural phenomenon so extraction can proceed unimpeded. Provocateur's
  natural ally: pushes for the boldest, most profitable resolution.
- **The Hush Choir** — a religious order that considers the Cathedral Spire
  district sacred and forbids disturbing it. Wants the Bell left an open
  question, or confirmed as a warning. Harmonist's natural ally: guards tonal
  and mythic register against being flattened into mundane explanation.

The Cartographers' Concord (official survey authority) and the Reef Wardens
(tide-savvy boat clans) exist as background texture the agents can reference
for spatial/procedural detail, but do not get their own dedicated seed
entries — cut to protect the SeedAgent's 6–8 entry budget for material that
actually pays off on screen.

## The deliberately contested seed entry

**The Drowned Bell.** Divers report a bell tone at slack low tide near the
submerged Cathedral Spire. The seed entry's status is explicitly
`contested`, with text along the lines of: *"Unconfirmed. No consistent
recording has ever been obtained. The Guild calls it wind-driven resonance.
The Choir calls it a warning. Official Concord position: disputed, not
resolved."*

This is the entry the Provocateur will be tempted to resolve too cleanly in
scene one (e.g. "confirmed mundane, safe to enter") — and the entry the
Lorekeeper cites by ID when it objects. This is the single moment the whole
demo's 1:00–2:00 beat is built around.

## Mid-generation human constraint injection (for the 2:00–2:20 demo beat)

> "The Concord's newly assigned surveyor is blind, and reads the drowned city
> by sound and current alone."

Not arbitrary: this is the one person in the story whose judgment about
whether the Bell is real actually matters, so the injected constraint has
narrative teeth rather than being a random fact bolted on.

---

## Why this was locked (rationale from the review pass)

**Demo legibility:** the core disagreement — *is the bell a hazard or a
hoax* — is a single sentence, legible cold, with zero setup required. The
demo script never gives factions their own explanation beat (only the four
*agents* get one line each at 0:12–0:30), so factions only need to be named
naturally once or twice as citation targets, not formally introduced.
Trimming from four factions to two was the one correction made after review,
specifically to avoid proper-noun overload in a 3-minute video with no
exposition budget, and to protect entry-budget for the Bell tension itself
rather than spending it on faction scene-setting.

**Thematic fit:** the cyanotype/ink-wash visual register isn't bolted onto
this premise, it's the native subject matter — cyanotype was literally
invented for ship blueprints and tide charts. The premise also satisfies the
demo-verification doc's own stated requirements (named factions, a scarce
resource, at least one ambiguous fact) almost as a direct checklist match,
and the tide-gated map gives the Architect real spatial-playability logic to
reason about even though that mechanic never needs to be narrated on screen.

**Honest limit:** a flooded gothic city with a mysterious bell and salvage
factions is a familiar genre space, not a novel one. That's treated as the
right tradeoff, not a flaw — per the project overview's own framing, the
Innovation & Creativity criterion has to rest on the *mechanism* (admission
gate, judge panel, Twee compiler), not the setting, and a recognizable genre
means judges spend zero cognitive budget decoding an unfamiliar world and
full attention watching the agents actually argue.
